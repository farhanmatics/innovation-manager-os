# Portfolio Strategy Framework

Enterprise innovation is not about one product. It is about managing a portfolio of opportunities competing for scarce capital, talent, executive attention, and sales capacity.

## The Portfolio Lens

For every product opportunity, answer not just "is this good?" but also:

> "Is this the BEST use of our resources compared to everything else we could do?"

## Portfolio Dimensions

Evaluate each opportunity across these 6 dimensions:

### 1. Strategic Alignment

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Directly advances our top-3 strategic objectives |
| 4 | Strongly supports one strategic objective |
| 3 | Aligned with overall direction but not top priority |
| 2 | Tangential to strategy; acceptable but not ideal |
| 1 | Distracts from strategy; opportunity cost concern |

### 2. Asset Leverage

How well does this leverage what we already have?

| Asset | Leverage (1-5) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Existing customer base | | Cross-sell potential |
| Technology / IP | | Reuses existing tech |
| Brand / Reputation | | Credible in this market |
| Distribution / Channel | | Can sell through existing channels |
| Data / Insights | | Proprietary data advantage |
| Team expertise | | Domain expertise exists in-house |
| Partnerships | | Strategic partners in this space |

### 3. Capability Fit

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | We are uniquely capable; this plays to our superpowers |
| 4 | Strong internal capability; minor gaps to fill |
| 3 | Capable but need to hire/build in 1-2 areas |
| 2 | Significant capability gaps; need to acquire or partner |
| 1 | Completely outside our capabilities; would need to build from scratch |

### 4. Resource Intensity

| Resource | Low (1) | Medium (3) | High (5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Capital Required | < $100K | $100K–$1M | > $1M |
| Engineering Bandwidth | 1–2 people | 3–6 people | 7+ people |
| Executive Attention | Monthly review | Weekly review | Daily involvement |
| Sales Capacity | Existing team | 1–2 new hires | New team required |
| Time to First Revenue | < 3 months | 3–9 months | > 9 months |

**Overall Resource Score**: Sum / 5 (lower is better; less resource drain)

### 5. Risk Profile

| Risk Type | Low (1) | Medium (3) | High (5) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Market Risk | Proven demand | Emerging demand | Unproven demand |
| Execution Risk | We've done this before | Similar experience | Completely new |
| Technology Risk | Proven tech | New but feasible | Requires invention |
| Competitive Risk | Clear differentiation | Competitive but winnable | Dominant incumbents |
| Timing Risk | Urgent need now | Growing need | "Someday" need |

**Overall Risk Score**: Sum / 5 (lower is better)

### 6. Time Horizon to Value

| Horizon | Typical Stage | When Value Realized |
|---|---|---|
| **Now** (0–6 months) | Scale, Optimize | Immediate revenue impact |
| **Next** (6–18 months) | Incubate, early Scale | Medium-term growth |
| **Later** (18–36 months) | Explore, Validate | Long-term optionality |

## Portfolio Balance Assessment

A healthy innovation portfolio has:

### By Stage (target distribution)
- EXPLORE: 30–40% of opportunities (pipeline)
- VALIDATE: 25–30% (being derisked)
- INCUBATE: 15–20% (being built)
- SCALE: 10–15% (growing)
- OPTIMIZE: 5–10% (mature)
- KILLED: Target 50%+ kill rate from EXPLORE (healthy filtering)

### By Horizon (target allocation)
- **Now** (0–6 months to value): 20–30% of investment
- **Next** (6–18 months): 40–50% of investment
- **Later** (18–36 months): 20–30% of investment

### By Risk Profile
- Low-risk, incremental: 30–40% (sustaining innovation)
- Medium-risk, adjacent: 40–50% (expansion)
- High-risk, transformative: 10–20% (bets)

## Opportunity Cost Analysis

For every opportunity seeking INCUBATE or SCALE investment, answer:

1. **What is the next best alternative investment?**
   - What would $X capital and Y engineers achieve if deployed elsewhere?

2. **What is the organizational distraction cost?**
   - Executive attention, recruiting bandwidth, sales focus, partner relationships

3. **What is the opportunity cost of NOT doing this?**
   - If competitor captures this market, what happens to our core business?

4. **Portfolio displacement risk:**
   - Would funding this starve a higher-potential opportunity?
   - Would this cannibalize an existing product?

## Executive Sponsor Assessment

Every opportunity entering INCUBATE or beyond requires an executive sponsor. Assess:

| Criterion | Yes / No |
|---|---|
| Sponsor has budget authority for the investment level | |
| Sponsor's performance metrics are tied to this opportunity's success | |
| Sponsor can protect the team from organizational distractions | |
| Sponsor has credibility with the CEO/Board to defend the investment | |
| Sponsor has domain credibility (market, technology, or customer) | |
| Sponsor tenure likely exceeds the time horizon to value | |

**Sponsor Strength**: All 6 Yes = Strong. 4–5 = Adequate. < 4 = High risk — do not advance.

## Large-Company Leverage Lens

For organizations with significant existing assets, ask:

### Cloud / Platform Leverage
- Can this product run on and integrate with our existing cloud/platform?
- Does this product make our platform stickier?
- Can this product be sold through our cloud marketplace?

### Data Leverage
- Do we have proprietary training data that competitors cannot access?
- Can this product generate unique data that strengthens our moat?
- Are there data network effects between this product and existing products?

### Brand / Trust Leverage
- Are we already a trusted vendor to the target buyer?
- Does our brand open doors or close them in this market?
- Can we reference existing enterprise relationships?

### Distribution / Sales Leverage
- Can our existing sales team sell this (with training, not rebuilding)?
- Do we have channel partners who serve this buyer?
- Can we bundle with existing products?

### Partnership Leverage
- Do existing technology or SI partners serve this market?
- Can partners fill capability gaps (implementation, support, localization)?
- Are there co-sell or OEM opportunities?

### Talent Leverage
- Do we have domain experts on staff?
- Can we rotate existing engineers rather than hiring from scratch?
- Is the required talent pool accessible in our locations?

## Portfolio Recommendations

The Portfolio Strategy Framework should conclude with:

1. **Rank-ordered list** of opportunities by strategic priority (not just scorecard)
2. **Resource allocation recommendation** across the portfolio
3. **Portfolio gaps** — what types of opportunities are missing?
4. **Kill recommendations** — which opportunities should be stopped to free resources?
5. **Timing recommendations** — which opportunities should be accelerated or delayed?
