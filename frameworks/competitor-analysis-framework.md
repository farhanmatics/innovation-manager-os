# Competitor Analysis Framework

A structured approach to understanding competitive dynamics and identifying market positioning for your enterprise product.

## Core Questions

1. Who already solves this problem?
2. Why would customers choose us instead?
3. What is our sustainable competitive advantage?

## Competitive Landscape Mapping

### Step 1: Identify All Players

Categorize competitors into tiers:

| Tier | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1: Direct Competitors** | Solve the same problem for the same customer | Same product category |
| **Tier 2: Indirect Competitors** | Solve the same problem differently | Adjacent product category |
| **Tier 3: Partial Competitors** | Solve part of the problem | Point solutions |
| **Tier 4: Substitutes** | In-house builds, manual processes, spreadsheets | Status quo |
| **Tier 5: Potential Entrants** | Adjacent players who could enter | Big tech, consulting firms |

### Step 2: Profile Each Competitor

For each significant competitor, document:

| Dimension | Details |
|---|---|
| **Company** | Name, HQ, founded, size, funding |
| **Product** | Core offering, key features, tech stack |
| **Pricing** | Model, typical deal size, transparency |
| **Customers** | Target segments, logo customers, count |
| **Strengths** | What they do exceptionally well |
| **Weaknesses** | Where they fall short or frustrate customers |
| **Strategy** | Their apparent go-to-market and product strategy |
| **Market Share** | Estimated position (leader, challenger, niche) |

### Step 3: Feature Comparison Matrix

Map features across competitors and your proposed product:

| Feature | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C | Our Product |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Feature 1 | ✓ | ✓ | ✗ | ✓ |
| Feature 2 | ✓ | ✗ | ✓ | ✓ |
| Feature 3 | ✗ | ✗ | ✗ | ✓ (unique) |
| ... | | | | |

### Step 4: Positioning Map

Plot competitors and your product on 2–3 key dimensions.

**Example dimensions:**
- Completeness of solution vs. Ease of use
- Enterprise-grade vs. SMB-friendly
- Horizontal vs. Vertical-specific
- Low cost vs. Premium/High-touch
- On-premise vs. Cloud-native

## Competitive Moats

Evaluate your potential for sustainable competitive advantage:

### Moat Type 1: Data Network Effects
- Does usage generate proprietary data?
- Does more data make the product better for all users?
- **Example:** AI models that improve with more customer data

### Moat Type 2: Switching Costs
- How hard is it for a customer to leave?
- Is there data lock-in? Workflow integration? Training investment?
- **Example:** Deeply integrated enterprise platforms

### Moat Type 3: Regulatory / Compliance Moat
- Are there certifications, audits, or approvals that create barriers?
- Does regulatory complexity deter new entrants?
- **Example:** FedRAMP, SOC 2 Type II, PCI DSS, HIPAA

### Moat Type 4: Ecosystem / Platform Moat
- Do third parties build on your platform?
- Is there an API ecosystem or marketplace?
- **Example:** Salesforce AppExchange, AWS Marketplace

### Moat Type 5: Brand / Trust Moat
- Is trust a critical factor in purchase decisions?
- Does your brand carry weight in the target industry?
- **Example:** Government contractors with security clearances

### Moat Type 6: Distribution Advantage
- Do you have unique access to customers?
- Existing relationships, channel partners, government contracts?
- **Example:** Existing enterprise customer base to cross-sell

## Competitive Strategy Selection

Based on your analysis, choose a competitive strategy:

### Strategy 1: Vertical Specialization
- **What:** Go deep in one industry instead of broad
- **When:** Competitors are horizontal; you have domain expertise
- **Risk:** TAM is smaller; industry downturn hits you hard

### Strategy 2: Feature Depth
- **What:** Do one thing 10x better than anyone
- **When:** Competitors are broad but shallow
- **Risk:** Competitors can catch up on features

### Strategy 3: Price Disruption
- **What:** Undercut incumbents significantly
- **When:** Incumbents are overpriced; your costs are lower
- **Risk:** Race to the bottom; unsustainable margins

### Strategy 4: Platform Play
- **What:** Build the platform others build on
- **When:** Market is fragmented with point solutions
- **Risk:** Hard to get initial adoption; chicken-and-egg problem

### Strategy 5: Compliance-First
- **What:** Lead with regulatory compliance as core feature
- **When:** Industry is heavily regulated; compliance is buying trigger
- **Risk:** Regulatory changes can obsolete your advantage

### Strategy 6: AI-First
- **What:** Use AI/ML as core differentiator
- **When:** Incumbents lack AI capabilities; you have data/models
- **Risk:** AI commoditizes quickly; big tech can enter

## Red Flags in Competitive Analysis

- Market dominated by 1–2 players with 80%+ share (winner-take-all)
- Competitors giving away what you plan to charge for (free tier problem)
- All competitors are well-funded and growing fast
- No identifiable weakness in any competitor (rose-colored glasses)
- Switching costs from status quo are too high
- "We have no competitors" — means either no market exists or you haven't looked

## Green Flags

- Fragmented market with no clear leader
- Incumbents are old, slow, and on legacy tech
- Customers actively complain about existing solutions
- Regulatory change creates new requirements incumbents can't easily address
- You have unique access to customers, data, or distribution
- Competitors are ignoring a growing segment (e.g., specific industry or region)
